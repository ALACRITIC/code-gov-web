Agency Compliances Dashboard

The Source Code Policy was issued on August 8, 2016 and requires both agencies and OMB to take specific actions within 90 and 120 days, as well as on an ongoing basis. This section is meant to help agencies meet those deadlines and boot up their overall source code strategy.

Up-to-date source code policy on digital strategy page (90-day requirement)

November 6, 2016 marked 90 days from the issuance of the policy.

  • Green:
    • Agency has posted an approved policy regarding compliance with the Federal Source Code Policy to its digital strategy page.
  • Yellow:
    • Agency has submitted a draft of its policy to OMB and is awaiting final approval from agency counterparts prior to posting the policy to its digital strategy page. Alternatively, agency has posted a paragraph on its digital strategy page describing where they are in the policy development process.
  • Red:
    • Agency has not posted an approved policy or a progress updated to its digital strategy page and has not submitted a draft policy to OMB.

Up-to-date enterprise code inventory (120-day requirement)

December 6, 2016 marked 120 days from the issuance of the policy.

  • Green:
    • Agency has successfully filled out its enterprise code inventory pursuant to the metadata schema on Code.gov without any errors or warnings.
  • Yellow:
    • Agency has filled out its enterprise code inventory pursuant to the metadata schema on Code.gov, but has produced at least one error or warning.
  • Red:
    • Agency has not filled out its enterprise code inventory pursuant to the metadata schema on Code.gov.
